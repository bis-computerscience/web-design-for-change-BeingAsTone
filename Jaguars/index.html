How are the Jaguars doing now?

Jaguar, the Amazon's main predator and the largest feline,

is facing a decline due to deforestation and agricultural activities.

Despite conservation efforts, jaguar populations are threatened

by hunting, habitat destruction, and human-wildlife conflict.

Chinese investment in Latin America is also increasing demand

for jaguar parts, leading to illegal hunting and poaching

in strongholds like the Amazon.

What will happen in the Amazon

When Jaguars Disappear?

Jaguars live in tropical lowlands, preferably close to water such as rivers and inland wetlands. The loss of the jaguar, the highest-level predator in an ecosystem, could significantly impact the population sizes of plant and animal species and the balance of the environment jaguars living, potentially leading to extinction of some low on the food chain species. Jaguars are crucial in controlling other species' populations, and their disappearance could lead to an overpopulation of rodents, which would eat more bugs and seeds, and decrease tree regeneration. The food chain is crucial for maintaining ecological balance.
