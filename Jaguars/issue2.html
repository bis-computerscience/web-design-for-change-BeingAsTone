Why is Amazon disappearing?

The Amazon rainforest is disappearing primarily due to deforestation driven by human activities such as agriculture, cattle ranching, logging, and infrastructure development. Large areas are cleared for soy plantations and cattle pastures, often through illegal logging and slash-and-burn techniques. Additionally, mining operations and the construction of roads and dams contribute to the destruction of the forest.

Why does this problem occur?

TThe Amazon rainforest is being destroyed mainly for economic reasons: expanding agriculture (soy and cattle), logging, mining, and infrastructure development. These activities provide significant profits and employment.
