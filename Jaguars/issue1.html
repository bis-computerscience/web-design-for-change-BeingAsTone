Trend of the Jaguar Population

What is the current population of jaguars?

The jaguar population has been on a downward trend since 1960. It appears that it has decreased drastically compared to the first time. It can be seen that while the number was well over 400,000, in 2014 there were less than 50,000

Why does this problem occur?

The main reason why the jaguar population is decreasing is because their habitats are gradually being reduced by humans. As there is no place to live, their food will also disappear, and as a result, many jaguars are dying.
