Jaguar Range & Distribution

Why are the Jaguars disappearing?

Jaguars in the Amazon are disappearing due to habitat loss from deforestation, which reduces their living space and prey availability. Additionally, they are threatened by illegal hunting and poaching for their fur and body parts. Human-wildlife conflicts also arise as jaguars encroach on agricultural lands, leading to retaliatory killings by farmers. These combined pressures significantly contribute to the decline in their population.

How can we solve this problem?

Individuals can help save jaguars in the Amazon by supporting conservation organizations, raising awareness, making sustainable consumption choices, advocating for policies protecting their habitat, and participating in local conservation efforts.
